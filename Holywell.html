About.

According to the Blue and John Crow Mountains (2016), "Holywell is located over 3,000 feet above sea level in the cool tropical mist of the Blue Mountains." Holywell serves as the main recreational area in the Blue Mountains and is home to a variety of Jamaica's endemic birds and plants such as: crested quail-dove, Jamaican tody, chestnut-bellied cuckoo- among others. Currently, Holywell has four operating trails: Oatley Mountain, Blue Mahoe, Waterfall and Shelter. The fifth trail known as Wag water or Dick's Pond has been shortened due to a hurricane which blocks the trail to Dick's Pond. There are ten (10) gazebos at Holywell with each named after a bird or plant at the park.

Currently, Holywell has four operating trails: Oatley Mountain, Blue Mahoe, Waterfall and Shelter. The fifth trail known as Wag water or Dick's Pond has been shortened due to a hurricane which blocks the trail to Dick's Pond. There are ten (10) gazebos at Holywell with each named after a bird or plant at the park.

Other features include a small waterfall, kids discovery zone, plant nursery where plants can be purchased, bird watching among other things. Holywell is surrounded by Maroon communities which help to contribute to the history of the park along with the Craighton Estate which was once a Great House but is now a coffee farm which offers tours to visitors. All of these things contribute to the history of Holywell thus creating a distinction between it and that of other parks found in Jamaica.
